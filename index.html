The principle behind my builds

One pedal, four sounds

Here is the trick I build into most of my pedals, shown on my OCD-style drive: the knobs that matter exist twice. A soft push button — or a MIDI message from your switcher — decides which of the two is live. Dial in two Drive and two Tone settings and one pedal covers your rhythm sound, your solo boost and everything in between. This demo actually works, go ahead:

🎸
Classic crunch — warm germanium grit, bright and cutting
01 · Turn

Drag the knobs up and down. The white knobs are preset one, the red-marked knobs below are preset two. The scope shows exactly what Drive and Tone do to your signal — more drive, more clipping; more tone, more bite.

02 · Tap

The small buttons under each pair switch presets. Red ring lit = the red knob is live. Drive and Tone switch independently, so two knob pairs quietly turn into four complete sounds. No pops, relay-switched.

03 · Send

Or don't touch the pedal at all. Tap a sound on the little controller and watch the CC message run down the TRS cable — the pedal flips presets from across the stage. That's the whole magic, and it fits in any pedal.

Behind the pedals

The workshop

CRAFFONARA FX is a one-man workshop in Austria. By day I design automotive electronics; by night the same discipline goes into guitar pedals — proper PCB layout, clean wiring, and measurements instead of guesswork.

The MIDI obsession started on my own pedalboard: too many great pedals, not enough feet. Since then I have been putting relay bypass and MIDI TRS sockets into anything with a footswitch, so one controller can run the whole board.

Every build ships with a spec sheet, MIDI implementation chart where relevant, and a photo log of the work done inside your pedal.
